Recommender systems are ubiquitous in on-line services to drive businesses.
And many sequential recommender models were deployed in these systems to
enhance personalization. The approach of using the transformer decoder as the
sequential recommender was proposed years ago and is still a strong baseline in
recent works. But this kind of sequential recommender model did not scale up
well, compared to language models. Quite some details in the classical
self-attentive sequential recommender model could be revisited, and some new
experiments may lead to new findings, without changing the general model
structure which was the focus of many previous works. In this paper, we show
the details and propose new experiment methodologies for future research on
sequential recommendation, in hope to motivate further exploration to new
findings in this area.
