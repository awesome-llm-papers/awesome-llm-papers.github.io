How can we teach a robot to predict what will happen next for an activity it
has never seen before? We address this problem of zero-shot anticipation by
presenting a hierarchical model that generalizes instructional knowledge from
large-scale text-corpora and transfers the knowledge to the visual domain.
Given a portion of an instructional video, our model predicts coherent and
plausible actions multiple steps into the future, all in rich natural language.
To demonstrate the anticipation capabilities of our model, we introduce the
Tasty Videos dataset, a collection of 2511 recipes for zero-shot learning,
recognition and anticipation.
